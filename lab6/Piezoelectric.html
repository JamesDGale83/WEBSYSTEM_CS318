The Piezoelectric Effect

This is a phenomenon where certain solid materials, like quartz crystals, generate an electric charge in response to applied mechanical stress (pressure). This is known as the direct piezoelectric effect. The effect is also reversible, meaning an applied electrical field can cause these materials to deform mechanically, which is called the converse piezoelectric effect. The effect is used in diverse applications, from lighters and loudspeakers to sensors, microbalances, and ultrasound.
